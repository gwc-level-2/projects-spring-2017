If you are interested in learning more about the issue of food deserts in Milwaukee and helping to provide fresh, healthful food to everyone, consider supporting these major local organizations.

Growing Power's goal as set out by founder Will Allen is a simple one: "to grow food, to grow minds, and to grow community." They grow food in a number of hoop houses and employ people who live in the community. Working to strive by growing food and a sustainable food system for their community.
The Victory Garden Initiative is a group that builds gardens for families and communities around Milwaukee. Victory Garden Initiative builds communities that grow their own food, creating a community-based, socially-just, environmentally-sustainable, nutritious food system for all.
Alice's Garden is a two-acre urban community garden in Milwaukee, Wisconsin that has garden plots for rent, as well as educational programs and events. In many ways, it is more than a garden to the community; it is an oasis. A leader at the garden named Monique Inez Liston said, "For the people who are born with a fighting spirit, who have remembered, always connected to the need to fight for what's right, working to reclaim and nourish cultural and family traditions connected to the place." 1
